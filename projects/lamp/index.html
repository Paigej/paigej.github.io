3D printed lamp


View the Files on Github

For the Lamp Assignment, I've decided to use Poul Henningsen and his work as an inspiration. He was a Danish designer, and is known for branding all of his work "PH."




The first week I wanted to prove out creating a node-graph in Grasshopper that could build these shapes based on a few parameters. I can set the radius of the inner circle, and then I create an arch on the edge of the circle, and use it to sweep the path of the circle to make the disc/bowl/shape.



I created the legs (inspired by a rocket!) to hold all the legs. I used an L shape that I would later cut out of the part that attached to the lamp, to use to snap into place.



I designed tabs that would friction fit into the top of the lamp. Rather than printing the entire piece, I iterated on just the top with the tabs. It took three prints to get the fix exactly right.



I pulled over peice from the Grasshopper definition to build out a lamp, and just cut out slots in the pieces so that they would be held onto the rocket. The piece at the bottom is to provide more stability to keeping all of those pieces tight.



From there I embarked on about 50 hours of printing. Here you can see where the L-shapes snap into the lamp. I scaled them by about half a millimeter for tolerance to make sure it would fit into the main piece.



Here is the lamp assembled! The black color absorbs a lot of the light. I originally wanted to print it in white, but didn't get the filament in time. Cool aesthetic, bad lamp.



All and all, I'm really happy about how it all fit together. I definitely learned some great lessons. I definitely should have focused more on where the light bulb sat, and how it would be redirecting light. As of now it shines straight through the lamp, when I would want it to disperse more.
