Final Project Proposal


I'm going to call my project, "trying to not be the worst upstairs neighbor."


.

I have a bird feeder that hangs above my deck. Unfortunately, the birds that visit are quite the messy eaters.



They drop bird seed all of the time on our deck, and unfortunately, the wood slats that are closer to the building are not covered all all of the way to the wall, so occasionally some birdseed falls down below onto the neighbors deck.



the very bottom of the bird feeder has screw threads. And then I got the idea, a 3D printing a tray to hang beneath and catch the seeds!


Here is a sketch of what I'm thinking so far. I learned my lesson with the lamp, that you don't want to print large objects that need support. Therefore I am going to attempt to make flat pieces that snap together to form an "upside down pyramid bowl." I also will need to design threads and how the pole connects to the walls.

Open Questions:
  • How will the walls snap together?
  • How can I print mesh from a solid using slicer settings?
  • How will I connect the pole to the pyramid bowl?
  • Do I want to allow the birds to perch on the bowl and eat the seeds that fell into it?
  • Will the mesh drain water and not seeds?
  • Should I finish the part with Epoxy? Is that bird safe?
