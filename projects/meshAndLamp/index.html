Meshes and Lamps


View the Files on Github

For this assignment, I combined a T-Rex Skull (here) and a Flexi T-Rex (here).


For the most part, I used "PointsOn" to manipulate the mesh and shrink the Flexi head so that the skull head would fit over it, and then I used "MeshBooleanUnion" and "Join" and place them together.



I struggled the most in this assignment with cutting up the mesh and stitching it back together. I didnt have a issue cutting, but struggled patching the mesh in any way. I mostly tried different functions under "MeshRepair"



In the end, after much frustration, I just moved the points around to avoid opening up the mesh in the first place.



In hindsight, it would be best to change the mesh on the Flexi T-Rex so that the larger head can snap on, and they can be printed separately. That way I dont end up printing all of this support.



For the Lamp Assignment, I've decided to use Poul Henningsen and his work as an inspiration. He was a Danish designer, and is known for branding all of his work "PH."



This week I wanted to prove out creating a node-graph in Grasshopper that could build these shapes based on a few parameters. I can set the radius of the inner circle, and then I create an arch on the edge of the circle, and use it to sweep the path of the circle to make the disc/bowl/shape. I still need to design how to attach them together, and how they attach to the lamp.
